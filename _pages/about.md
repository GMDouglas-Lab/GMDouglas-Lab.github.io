*The Douglas Lab will be forming at the University of New Brunswick in July, 2025!*

I am currently looking for students (at any level) and postdocs who might be interested in joining my group. If you think you might be interested, or if you would like to discuss potential collaborations, or ideas more generally, then please contact me at .

My lab will use primarily bioinformatics approaches to explore the evolutionary factors driving pangenome evolution in prokaryotes. One direction will to build upon my [recent work](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41559-023-02268-6) using pseudogenes as a neutral reference to gain insight into how rare genes evolve in general. There are many questions related to this and related work that I would be excited to explore, such as how to best estimate effective population size from microbial phylogenomic data to better inform such investigations.

We will be exploring strain variation across reference genomes and in natural communities. For the latter, we will be working with collaborators to better understand the factors driving strain variation across ocean and lake water samples, as well as honey bee gut microbiome samples.

Last, we will develop bioinformatic tools to better identify putatively adaptive accessory genes, and to aid microbial ecology analyses more generally.
