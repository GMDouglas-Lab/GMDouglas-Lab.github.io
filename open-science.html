Peer Community In

I contribute to open access research dissemination as a Managing Board Member of Peer Community in Genomics. This is a platform for reviewing and recommending preprints. I am also the representative to the wider Peer Community In group (which includes numerous disciplines) for Peer Community in Genomics.

I am always happy to discuss the Peer Community In model, whether informally or via a formal presentation for any individuals or organizations interested. Please reach out if you want to learn about or discuss this model!

Publishing philosophy

I am passionate about improving publishing norms in research and our lab is committed to thoughtfully considering where we publish. When the choice of publication venue is solely ours (which is not always the case in large collaborations), we will avoid Gold open access journals, except for those affiliated with non-profits or research societies. Gold open access journals are those that rely entirely on article processing charges (i.e., where authors pay fees). My concern is that this business model incentivizes journals to accept a higher proportion of submissions, potentially including low-quality articles. To be clear, that is not to say that all Gold open access journals are low-quality or actually suffer from this bias, merely that the financial incentive exists, even if the individuals involved are well-intentioned. Assessing the extent to which this theorized bias exists, especially across diverse sub-fields, is challenging, and there are some inconsistent signals. In particular, there are no clear differences in citation rates between Gold open access and other journals in tested disciplines. Nonetheless, at least in one case Gold open access journals in economics have been shown to be much more likely to accept fake low-quality manuscripts compared to traditional journals.
