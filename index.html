My research group was established in July 2025 in the Biology Department at the University of New Brunswick, Fredericton campus.

We use bioinformatics approaches to explore the factors driving gene content diversity across bacteria. This builds upon my work using pseudogenes as a neutral reference to investigate how rare genes evolve. A key question I am excited to explore is how to best estimate the relative importance of natural selection vs. genetic drift in bacterial populations to better inform such investigations.

We also work with collaborators to better understand the factors driving strain variation across a range of environmental samples (including ocean and lake water samples, as well as honey bee gut microbiome samples).

Last, we develop bioinformatic tools to better identify putatively adaptive accessory genes, and to aid microbial ecology analyses more generally. We will especially focus on developing approaches that incorporate genomes (both high-quality reference and metagenome-assembled genomes) into metagenomic analyses.
